Wat Pah Bodhirama

Welcome to Wat Pah Bodhirama

Wat Pah Bodhirama is a Buddhist monastery located in Bali, Indonesia, dedicated to the practice and dissemination of the Dhamma in the Forest Tradition. This website serves as a resource for those interested in learning from the teachings of Ajahn Visalo and participating in the life of the monastery.

Here you will find information about upcoming events, Dhamma talks, and guidelines for visiting and practicing with the community. Our aim is to provide a clear and inspiring introduction to the practice of mindfulness and compassion.

WAT PAH BODHIRAMA

Bodhirama Forest Monastery

Wat Where Monks Live
Forest Dad
Bodhi = Enlightenment
Arama = Garden, Forest
Bodhi + Arama = Bodhirama

It is a forest where the Bhikkhu Sangha lives to practice Dhamma in silence. And to guard the Vinaya, namely the 227 Bhikkhu Precepts passed down by the Buddha to the Bhikkhu Sangha. Where the Dhamma and Vinaya are used by the Bhikkhu Sangha to erode inner defilements.

In this regard, Wat Pah Bodhirama refers to the Theravada Forest tradition in Thailand, following the teachings of Ajahn Cha, which emphasizes the Dhutanga/ascetic rules of monks. This serves as a training tool and way of life for the Bhikkhu Sangha, in developing Sila, Samadhi, and Panya.

Wat Pah Bodhirama also offers opportunities for devotees who wish to experience and practice in simplicity and silence. Wat Pah Bodhirama is a place to practice Dhamma and Vinaya while blending with nature. Take the life of our Great Teacher, Gautama Buddha, as an example. He was born under a tree, attained enlightenment under a tree, and attained Parinibbana under a tree.
