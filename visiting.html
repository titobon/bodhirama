Visiting Wat Pah Bodhirama

Welcome

We welcome those who wish to visit and practice in a monastic environment. To ensure a peaceful and harmonious atmosphere for all residents and visitors, we ask that you read and respect the following guidelines.

Code of Conduct

  • Dress Code: Please dress modestly. Clothing should cover the shoulders, chest, and knees. Avoid transparent, tight-fitting, or revealing clothing. White or other plain, light-colored clothing is recommended.
  • Noble Silence: We observe Noble Silence, especially in meditation areas and during designated times. Please keep conversations to a minimum and speak softly when necessary.
  • Etiquette: Please be respectful of the monks, nuns, and fellow lay practitioners. Follow traditional customs such as bowing when appropriate.

Accommodations

Accommodations are simple and in line with a monastic lifestyle. Men and women have separate lodging areas. Please be prepared for basic facilities.

Daily Schedule

The daily schedule typically includes group meditation sessions, chanting, work periods, and Dhamma talks. A detailed schedule will be provided upon arrival. Punctuality is expected.

Example Schedule:

  • 4:00 AM: Morning Chanting and Meditation
  • 6:00 AM: Breakfast
  • 8:00 AM: Work Period
  • 11:00 AM: Main Meal
  • 5:00 PM: Evening Chanting and Meditation

What to Bring

Please bring your own toiletries, a towel, a flashlight, an alarm clock, and any necessary medications. Bedding is provided. Valuables should be kept to a minimum.
